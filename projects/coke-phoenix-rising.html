Coke Phoenix Rising

Lead DevOps Engineer & Security Automation Specialist · Coca-Cola / TCCC (via Umbrage)
SHEET: A-04
PLATFORM: Azure DevOps
DATE: Jan–Mar 2025

Designed and built the infrastructure and CI/CD pipeline for Coca-Cola's Phoenix Rising platform from the ground up, then handed the design to Coke's own architecture and security teams to adopt and run.

The Challenge

Infrastructure that had to pass Coke's own bar

Phoenix Rising needed infrastructure and a deployment pipeline built from nothing — dev, QA, and production, plus a fully mirrored disaster recovery environment — with a security posture that could pass Coke's own architecture and security review, not just Umbrage's internal standard.

Solution Architecture

Build it, then bring in the reviewers

Designed and built the Terraform infrastructure and Azure DevOps CI/CD pipelines internally first — environments for dev, QA, and production, plus a mirrored disaster recovery environment. Took that design to Coke's architecture team and security review board, worked through their review directly, and came out with ongoing DR design and approval authority for the application. On top of that foundation: automated certificate and service-principal lifecycle management to eliminate manual renewals, linked Azure DevOps to the legacy TFS reporting repo, and ran a multi-session training program for Accenture — the vendor taking over day-to-day support — and TCCC.

Key Accomplishments

Outcomes

  • Eliminated certificate expiration incidents entirely through automated renewal

Lessons Learned

What held up

Building the design internally before bringing it to the review board sped up alignment rather than slowing it down — there was a concrete artifact to react to instead of a blank whiteboard. Automation beat documentation alone for actually eliminating recurring certificate and secret-rotation incidents.
